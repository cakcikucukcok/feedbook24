Chapter 8

We will Exchange the Cross Some Day for a Crown

Remember Jesus died on the cross for you and me to redeem, to recover, deliver from sin and damnation. The cross symbolizes death, pain, life, suffering, shame and disgrace. Whenever the word life shows up, we get very excited because there is always hope for a brighter future. Life is good but it comes with pain, suffering and shame. Jesus went through all the suffering and came out, victorious and a conqueror. Jesus is the winner all the time and Jesus wants us to carry our cross, to come out as pure gold and we have to be triumphant like he was. God has given us greater power to carry the cross like Jesus.

And the seven angels which had the seven trumpets prepared themselves to sound. Revelation 8:6

We need to prepare ourselves for the coming of the Lord Jesus to be a true disciple. We ought to always be ready to go through pain and suffering. And whosoever doth not bear his cross, and come after me, cannot be my disciple. (Luke 14: 27)

Remember some day we will exchange the cross for a crown and without the cross; we cannot receive the crown. Jesus promised to see us through our tribulations. Give him a chance to carry us through and he will not fail, why not give Jesus a chance today in your life?

Seek him while you are still healthy and alive, before its too late. Jesus is the answer for the world today. Some of the Church leaders are facing difficulty with answering the worlds questions. They need our prayer and fasting. Jesus is the only problem solver in our life.

There are three Crosses and each cross Represents: 1. Redeem

2. Repentance

3. Rebellious

Jesus is in the middle and the two thieves represent the other two persons.

Then were there two thieves crucified with him, one on the right hand, and another on the left. (Matthew 27:38)

Jesus cross-symbolizes redemption, regain, possession to deliver from sin and set free from all addiction in our life. The cross that Jesus was crucified on is very important. He could have called ten thousand angels and set himself free, but it was his desire to do his fathers will to accomplish our salvation.
