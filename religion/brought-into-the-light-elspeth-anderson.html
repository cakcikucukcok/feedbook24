At present the LDS Church is struggling to capture the hearts and minds of Millennials. Rather than telling them who they are, it would be better to allow them to express who they are. Elspeth Anderson's intensely raw and personal spiritual journey provides such an opportunity. It is an interesting and thoughtful primary source that will be valuable in understanding how Millennials are grappling with the complexities of life given their unique generational makeup. Her experiences and how she processes them provides a window into the importance of understanding one's spiritual experiences in light of competing narratives and how tradition has often hampered new insights. Her work will also assist others struggling to make sense of the ups and downs of life with an eye toward the spiritual. -E. Jay Richardson, PhD
