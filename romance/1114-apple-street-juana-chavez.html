Does tragedy define a person? In the aftermath of loss, can we ever hope to start over? Jason O’Brien is trying to start over. After the death of his wife, he lives a routine life boarding neurosis. Some might say he's not really living, but for Jason, monotony is security. He's safe now, but alone, very much alone. Until, one day a simple mistake sets him on a path causes him to question his comfortable perspective. True Garcia, a journalism major, has an assignment to write a story about a news event that occurred on the day she was born. She finds Christine Michaels, a young mother who died in an unexplained shooting. True's search for more information regarding Christine Michaels’ death leads her to Professor Jason O’Brien. True and Jason set out to learn what happened to a woman that touched both their lives through her death, yet their own lives’ tragedies bring them together unexpected ways. Jason is brought to spiritual awareness through True’s belief in Jesus Christ and her large loving family. The mystery within his own soul is revealed as he discovers the end of his loneliness is found in a surprising new relationship.
